Overall Draft Strategy

Your goal in this draft is to build a well-balanced and deep roster by focusing on the best available players at RB and WR in the early rounds. Start by securing top-tier talent at both positions in Rounds 1-2, ensuring you leave with at least one elite RB or WR. In Rounds 3-4, continue to build balance, but be open to drafting an elite QB or TE if they fall to you. As the draft progresses, prioritize depth and upside, targeting high-ceiling players who can break out or provide solid backup options. Be flexible and prepared to adapt to unexpected scenarios, such as runs on QBs or TEs, and always keep an eye on value. By the end of the draft, you should have a strong starting lineup with quality depth at key positions, ready to compete throughout the season.

Fantasy Draft Plan - 12th Pick, 12-Team PPR

Rounds 1-2 (Picks 12 & 13)

Priority: Best Player Available (RB/WR)
Considerations:
  • Top RBs to consider: Christian McCaffrey, Bijan Robinson, Breece Hall.
  • Top WRs to consider: Tyreek Hill, Justin Jefferson, Ja'Marr Chase, Amon-Ra St. Brown.
  • If these top-tier players are taken, look at next tier: Garrett Wilson, CeeDee Lamb.
  • If RBs and WRs are heavily drafted, prioritize the best available regardless of position.
Must: Draft two of the best RB/WRs available. Leave this round with a balanced RB/WR duo or two elite WRs.
Avoid: Do not draft a QB or TE. Focus only on RBs and WRs.

Rounds 3-4 (Picks 36 & 37)

Priority: Balance Your Roster (RB/WR)
Considerations:
  • If you started with 1 RB and 1 WR, maintain balance by drafting another RB and WR.
  • Next best RBs: Jahmyr Gibbs, Travis Etienne Jr., Jonathan Taylor.
  • Next best WRs: Jaylen Waddle, A.J. Brown, Chris Olave, Michael Pittman Jr.
  • If you started with 2 WRs, prioritize drafting a RB like Saquon Barkley or Kyren Williams.
  • If you started with 2 RBs, prioritize a WR like Garrett Wilson or Davante Adams.
  • If Josh Allen or Jalen Hurts is somehow still available, consider drafting them.
Must: Ensure you have at least two RBs and two WRs by the end of this round.
Avoid: Reaching for a QB or TE unless it’s a clear value pick. Continue focusing on building depth at RB and WR.

Rounds 5-6 (Picks 60 & 61)

Priority: Fill Gaps and Add Depth
Considerations:
  • High-upside RBs: Isiah Pacheco, De'Von Achane, Rachaad White.
  • High-upside WRs: DJ Moore, Deebo Samuel, Tee Higgins, DeVonta Smith.
  • If you lack a TE, this is a good spot to target a TE like Sam LaPorta or Mark Andrews.
  • If you still need a QB, consider drafting Patrick Mahomes II, Lamar Jackson, or Jalen Hurts if still available.
Must: Continue building depth at RB and WR. Ensure you have a strong bench and potential starters.
Avoid: Reaching for lower-tier QBs or TEs unless you’ve missed out on top targets.

Rounds 7-8 (Picks 84 & 85)

Priority: High-Upside Players
Considerations:
  • Target players with high upside: WRs like George Pickens, Zay Flowers, or RBs like James Cook.
  • If you still don’t have a QB or TE, this is a good spot to grab one like Kirk Cousins or Dalton Kincaid if they’re still available.
  • If you've been waiting on TE, Trey McBride and Evan Engram are good value targets.
Must: Draft players who can provide value during bye weeks or have breakout potential.
Avoid: Drafting players with low ceilings or uncertain roles unless they are clear handcuffs.

Rounds 9-10 (Picks 108 & 109)

Priority: Backup QB/TE and Bench Depth
Considerations:
  • Backup QB targets: Dak Prescott, Tua Tagovailoa, or Jared Goff if you don't have a starter yet.
  • TEs like Kyle Pitts or David Njoku could be valuable if you haven’t secured one.
  • Consider handcuffing your top RBs if the handcuff is still available (e.g., Zamir White for Josh Jacobs).
Must: Solidify your bench with reliable players who can start in a pinch.
Avoid: Drafting defenses or kickers. Focus on depth and securing potential starters.

Rounds 11-12 (Picks 132 & 133)

Priority: High-Upside Bench Players
Considerations:
  • Look for high-upside WRs like Jaxon Smith-Njigba or RBs like Javonte Williams.
  • Target players who could benefit from an injury ahead of them on the depth chart.
Must: Draft players with high upside who can outperform their draft position.
Avoid: Players in crowded or unclear situations without clear paths to playing time.

Rounds 13-14 (Picks 156 & 157)

Priority: Defense and Depth
Considerations:
  • Consider drafting a defense if one of the top defenses (e.g., 49ers, Cowboys) is available.
  • If all defenses are similar, consider taking another high-upside bench player instead.
Must: Secure a starting defense or add to your bench depth.
Avoid: Reaching for a kicker. Leave kicker selection to the final round.

Rounds 15-16 (Picks 180 & 181)

Priority: Kicker and Final Bench Spot
Considerations:
  • Draft your kicker in Round 16. Target top options like Justin Tucker or Harrison Butker.
  • In Round 15, consider a final high-upside bench player like Marvin Mims Jr. or a handcuff if available.
Must: Finish your draft with a reliable kicker and a player with potential to contribute or replace a starter.
Avoid: Drafting based on name recognition or reaching for players with low upside.

Handling Unusual Draft Scenarios

Scenario 1: Early QB Run

If multiple QBs are drafted early (within the first 3 rounds), stay calm. Continue focusing on RBs and WRs.
Target QBs like Lamar Jackson or Jalen Hurts in Rounds 4-5 if available. If they’re gone, wait and draft a QB in Rounds 7-8, focusing on depth at RB/WR first.

Scenario 2: TE Run

If multiple TEs go off the board early, don’t panic. Focus on drafting the best available RBs and WRs.
If all elite TEs are gone by Round 4, wait until Rounds 7-8 to draft a TE like Dalton Kincaid or Evan Engram. Prioritize depth over reaching for a lower-tier TE.

Scenario 3: All Your Targets Are Gone

If you reach a round and all your target players are drafted, pivot to the next best player available. Stay flexible.
Keep a list of backup targets in each round. Focus on players in strong offenses or with clear paths to playing time. Don’t hesitate to take the next tier down if your original targets are gone.

Scenario 4: Injury-Prone Players

If you draft an injury-prone player early (e.g., Christian McCaffrey, Saquon Barkley), prioritize their handcuff in the later rounds.
Consider handcuffing these players around Rounds 9-10 if available. This will protect your investment and give you peace of mind.

Scenario 5: Unexpected Drop

If an unexpected player drops significantly (e.g., Josh Allen or Travis Kelce in Round 3), consider pivoting your strategy to take advantage of the value.
If you grab a QB/TE early due to a drop, adjust by focusing on RB/WR depth in the next few rounds to balance your roster.
